## The audience

I decided to make my audience local residents of Pittsburgh, who may not be familiar with the specifics of the U.S., prison system. I thought this would give me the chance to focus on a different side of this topic. I was able to utilize international, national, and state data as well as the real life stories of artists from PA but a few from Pittsburgh, allowing me to employ some personal connections to the story. 

## Final design decisions

I felt my title slide should give an overview of the entire research topic rather than an ominous beginning, so I changed the transition to go from the exterior of prison to an art work. After the presentation, I decided to make some of the suggested changes such as having more of a transition from the incarceration topic to the arts intervention. Included a slide addressing potential societal fixes to the current prison system, and how the arts can be of equal service. Based on my colleague's presentations, I also felt I was missing contextual paragraphs, so I included some around the visualizations. I also added a local Pittsburgh organization to the impact stories page, 1Hood Media Academy, that works with in art and activism, with specific programs on prison reform. Additionally, I moved around the national and Pennsylvania data in a way that flowed better. I also added one more data visualization of full world prison population at the beginning to help ground context of the presentation.
